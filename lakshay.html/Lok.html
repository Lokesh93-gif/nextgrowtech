A "puncher car" isn't a standard term, but it almost certainly refers to a car with a puncture, meaning a hole in a tire, usually from a sharp object like a nail or glass, causing it to lose air and go flat or slow. People often say "punctured" or "flat tire," but "puncher" is a common informal way, especially in some regions, to describe the issue with the car (the puncture itself) or the action of puncturing.
